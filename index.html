About Me

I am a Prime Minister's Research Fellow (PMRF) at the Indian Institute of Technology Jammu, pursuing a PhD in the Department of Physics. My research lies at the exciting intersection of non-equilibrium statistical physics and molecular biology.

I focus on understanding the regulation of gene expression, genomics, and transcriptomics through the lens of physical bioinformatics. My goal is to develop quantitative models that explain complex cellular processes, such as the optimization of cellular resources and protein synthesis under stress conditions.

Research Interests

Non-equilibrium statistical physics

Applying physical principles to understand dynamic biological systems.

Gene expression regulation

Modeling the complex kinetics of transcription and translation.

Optimization of cellular resources

Investigating how cells efficiently manage protein synthesis.

Physical Bioinformatics

Developing computational frameworks for biological data.
